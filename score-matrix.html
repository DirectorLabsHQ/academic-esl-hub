What is CEFR?

The Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) is an international standard for describing language ability. It describes language proficiency on a six-point scale, from A1 for beginners, up to C2 for those who have mastered a language.

Most universities require a B2 or C1 level for international students to ensure they can handle academic lectures and assignments.

How to use this matrix

This matrix helps you understand where you stand if you have taken one test but need to provide a score for another. For example, if you have an IELTS 6.5, you can see that you likely fall into the B2 category, which is roughly equivalent to a TOEFL score of 79-93.

Note: Scores are approximate and vary by institution. Always check the official requirements of your target university.
