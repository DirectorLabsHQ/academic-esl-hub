    Expert Insights: Why Exam Scores Differ

    Integrated vs. Independent

    The TOEFL iBT often uses "integrated tasks," measuring synthesized academic ability. IELTS tends to keep skills more independent. This causes variance for students with uneven skill sets.

    Human vs. AI Scoring

    OPIc measures functional fluency via adaptive conversation. TOEIC focuses on workplace context. High conversational fluency doesn't always translate to high academic test scores.

    The B2 Academic Ceiling

    Most universities require IELTS 6.5 or TOEFL 80 (B2/C1 boundary). Students below this threshold often spend 50% more time on reading assignments than native peers.

    The CEFR Yardstick

    The Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) tells institutions exactly what you can do in a real-world academic environment.

    Achieving a B2 Level is the "tipping point" where a student can follow lectures without constant cognitive overload.
