Why These Tools Matter

Vocabulary and Academic Reading

University textbooks and articles use a lot of specific words. Studies show you usually need to know about 8,000–10,000 words (especially academic ones) to read most texts comfortably—meaning you understand 95–98% of the words without stopping too often.

If your vocabulary feels a bit small right now, that's normal for many learners. The good news is you can grow it step by step. This tool gives you a clear starting point so you know what to focus on.

Reading Speed and Comprehension

When reading is slow, studying takes longer and feels tiring. It can also make it harder to finish exams on time or understand long readings deeply. A comfortable academic reading speed is usually 250–350+ words per minute.

This tool uses passages similar to real university materials so the result is useful. If your speed is lower than you'd like, don't worry—short, regular practice makes a big difference fast.

Our Approach

The tools follow CEFR standards and research on academic English and second language learning. They are designed to give honest, helpful feedback—not to replace official language tests.

Everything here is built to support real students and teachers working toward real academic goals.
