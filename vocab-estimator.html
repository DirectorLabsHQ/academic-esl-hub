The Science of Vocabulary Measurement

The 8,000 Word Threshold

For ESL learners, reaching a vocabulary size of 8,000 to 9,000 word families is a critical milestone. This level of lexical knowledge allows for "unassisted reading" of academic journals, newspapers, and classic literature.

At this stage, a reader typically understands 98% of the words in a text. This 98% lexical coverage is the scientifically recognized threshold required for a reader to comfortably infer the meaning of unknown words from context without needing a dictionary.

CEFR & Academic Success

Our estimator aligns your results with the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). While an A1 level typically requires 500 words, a C2 (Mastery) level requires a range of 10,000 to 16,000 words.

This test focuses specifically on the "Academic Gap"—the transition between B2 (Upper Intermediate) and C1 (Advanced). This is where most international students struggle when entering English-medium universities, as the complexity of academic vocabulary increases exponentially compared to conversational English.
