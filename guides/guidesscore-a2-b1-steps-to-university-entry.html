A2–B1 Score:
Your Path to University

Starting at an A2 or B1 level is perfectly normal. While most universities require a B2 (Upper-Intermediate) for full admission, you can bridge this gap in roughly 6 months with a disciplined plan.

1. Understanding the Goal

"University Entry" means you can follow a lecture without a translator. Here is what you need:

  • B2 Proficiency: The ability to understand complex ideas and speak fluently with native speakers.
  • Pathway Programs: Many universities offer "Foundation" years if you are at a B1 level.
  • Test Scores: Target an IELTS 6.0 or TOEFL 80.

2. Your 6-Month Roadmap

Phase 1: Months 1–2

Foundations & Immersion

Focus on high-frequency vocabulary and grammar. Switch your phone and social media to English.

Phase 2: Months 3–4

Academic Transition

Start reading news articles and writing 300-word summaries. Listen to "English Learning" podcasts daily.

Phase 3: Months 5–6

Exam Readiness

Take timed practice tests. Focus on academic essay structure and formal speaking drills.

3. Daily Skill Integration

Reading

Read "Graded Readers" at Level 3 or 4. Do not use a dictionary for every word.

Listening

Watch TED-Ed videos. They have clear pronunciation and academic topics.
