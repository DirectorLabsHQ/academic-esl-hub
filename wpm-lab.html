Reading Fluency & WPM Lab

In academic English, fluency is your ability to process written information quickly and accurately. This Reading Speed Lab helps you find your current Words Per Minute (WPM) using three levels of academic text.

Wait! Why does reading speed matter? High-level exams like the IELTS and TOEFL require you to read long, complex articles in a very short time. If you read too slowly, you won't finish the test. If you read too fast, you might miss important details.

Our lab uses a variety of randomized passages. This means you can return often to practice different topics, ensuring your speed is consistent across various academic subjects like science, history, and technology.

How to read faster

The secret to reading faster is chunking. Instead of looking at every word, try to see groups of 3-4 words at once. This reduces the strain on your eyes and helps your brain process complete ideas faster.

A good target for academic reading is 200+ WPM with 80% comprehension. If your speed is high but your score is low, try slowing down and focusing on key words like however, therefore, or furthermore that show the logic of the story.

Student Tip

For ESL students, the biggest barrier to speed is vocabulary. If you don't know 95% of the words in a text, your speed will drop because you have to stop and think about each word. Use our Vocabulary Test to check your level!
